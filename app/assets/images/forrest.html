This Spring discover the wonders of Ecclesall Woods

Added on: 16th March, 2012

Add Comment
  • Print

It is a sorry state of affairs when people have to be encouraged to experience the great outdoors. Kids would rather play the latest games console then discover the wonders of the natural world that lies right on their doorstep. Well, if you go down to Eccelsall Woods this Spring you will find a lot more than just some peckish teddy bears. The new JG Graves Woodland Discovery Centre finally opened at the end of last year and aims to tear kids away from the TV screen and introduce them to their local environment.

Eccelsall Woods is the largest semi-natural woodland in South Yorkshire, attracting 300,000 visitors a year. The local nature reserve is home to several important UK species and is also the location of some historic monuments. Yet the woods have never had a proper visitors centre; until now.

Work on the JG Graves Woodland Discovery Centre began way back in 2008. The aim of the centre is to educate people about the importance of preserving the woodland and promoting the local environment. The building itself aims to be as eco friendly as possible. The spectacular structure is made almost entirely from locally-sourced timber and includes a wood burner and toilet cubicles doors made from recycled plastic.

The centre plans to house a vast range of events and activities to encourage people to step outside and visit the ancient woodland. The centre includes an indoor classroom and two outdoor areas in which schools are invited to come down and take part in activities. Local supporter, Graham Howard, says It is a fun and insightful way to teach children about the importance of looking after the local environment and wildlife.

There is also a variety of craft courses available throughout the year and a number of events such as woodland walks and exhibitions planned. Volunteer projects, such as The Sheffield Woodlands Environment Enhancement Project (SWEEP) are also based at the centre. They encourage people to come down and help maintain the woodland and preserve the habitats of local wildlife.

Finally, for those who really wish to get back to nature, why not hold your wedding inside the breathtaking Sycamore Suite. This Spring you will be able to say your vows surrounded by the beautiful woodland setting and then head back inside this incredible building for the reception.

So, put down your X-Box and step away from your laptop. With warm weather on its way, this is the ideal chance to head down to Eccelsall Woods and experience the beauty of a blossoming Spring.
By Adam Nash
